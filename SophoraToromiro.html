Sophora toromiro

Sophora toromiro, also known as the toromiro tree, is an iconic and endangered tree on Easter Island in the remote South Pacific. This small tree with fern-like leaves was once present on the island and was an important cultural element for the Rapa Nui Aboriginal people. However, due to centuries of deforestation and environmental changes, toromiro has disappeared from the wild, being cultivated and propagated only in botanical gardens around the world.
