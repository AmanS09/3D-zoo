Zebra

Zebras are herbivorous mammals known for their distinctive black and white striped fur, which serves as a form of camouflage and protection from predators in their native African grasslands and savannahs. They belong to the equine family and are characterized by an upright mane and a long tail with a tuft of hair at the tip. Zebras are social animals that usually live in groups or "dazzles" consisting of a dominant male, several females and their offspring. Their primary diet is grasses and they have adapted to a nomadic lifestyle, constantly searching for fresh pastures. These animals have excellent hearing and vision, which helps them detect predators such as lions and hyenas. Although there are three distinct species of zebra – plains zebra, Grevy's zebra and mountain zebra – they all face different conservation challenges due to habitat loss and poaching, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts for these iconic African animals.
