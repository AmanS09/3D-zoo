Elephant

Elephants are the largest animals in the world and are known for their size, long noses and unique ears. They include two species: African elephants and Asian elephants, which are savannah and forest subspecies. These intelligent, social people have a social structure governed by matrilineal families and are carnivorous; They eat a wide variety of plants, including grass, leaves, and bark. Their noses and upper lips are fused together, giving them great flexibility in breathing, communicating, eating, and controlling things. Elephants play an important role in ecosystems as seed dispersers and habitats, but they face significant conservation challenges such as habitat loss, ivory trafficking and human conflict.
