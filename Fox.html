Fox

Foxes are small to medium-sized mammals with pointed ears, bushy tails, and long fur. They are omnivores, meaning they eat both meat and plants. Foxes have a flattened skull, a pointed snout, and sharp teeth. They are about 32 to 57 inches (81 to 145 centimeters) long, including their tail. Foxes usually weigh 6 to 17 pounds (3 to 8 kilograms). Foxes are members of the Canidae family, which also includes jackals, wolves, and dogs. They are often mistaken for other members of the Canidae family because of their long, thin legs, lithe frame, and bushy tail. Foxes live on every continent except Antarctica. They thrive in cities, towns, and rural settings
