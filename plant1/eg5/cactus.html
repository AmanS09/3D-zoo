Fishhook barrel Cactus

Fishhook barrel cactus (Ferocactus wislizeni) is a succulent plant that can grow 6 to 10 feet tall and 18 to 30 inches in diameter. It has a large, ribbed, barrel-like body and thick hook-like spines. Its famous name comes from its hook-like needle. The homeland of the Fishing Barrel Cactus is the arid desert regions of the world. It can be found in the Sonoran desert and Chihuahua. It likes full sun in sandy, well-drained soil. It can grow outdoors but cannot withstand cold. The lifespan of the fishing cactus is 50-100 years. It has bright orange-yellow flowers in September and October. It also produces yellow berry-like fruits.
