Jade Plant

Magnolia, also known as powder grass or silver grass, is a succulent plant native to South Africa and Mozambique. It is a popular houseplant because it is easy to grow and easy to care for at home. The jade tree grows slowly and reaches a height of 3 to 6 feet. They have thick leaves and stems that store water in their roots, and oval leaves. They produce clusters of small white or pink star-like flowers. Jade trees like it hot, dry and grow well in containers. They like a lot of light and should be placed in south, east or west windows. A jade plant placed in low light will become heavy and leggy. With proper care, a jade tree can live up to 70 years. It is best to reapply every 2-3 years. When repotting, roots should be trimmed and stems trimmed to stimulate stem growth.
