Rhododendron

Rhododendron is a genus of flowering plants that includes over 1,000 species. The name comes from the Greek words “rhodo” (rose) and “dendron” (tree). Rhododendrons are native to the temperate regions of Asia, North America, and Europe, as well as the tropical regions of southeast Asia and northern Australia. They can be either evergreen or deciduous and range in size from low-growing ground covers to tall trees. Some species are only 10 cm (4 inches) high. Rhododendrons are found in two categories: cultivated and native plants. Native plants are found in moist or wet woods, sometimes in dense colonies. The Darjeeling and Sikkim Himalayas are home to more than one-third of all types of rhododendrons found in India. Rhododendrons are the national flower of Nepal and the state tree of Uttarakhand and state flower of Nagaland in India.
