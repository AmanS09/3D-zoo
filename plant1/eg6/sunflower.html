Sunflower

Sunflowers are annual plants with large flowers that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The flower head has many small flowers. The outer leaves are called ligulate. Sunflower got its name because its flowers follow the sun from east to west. Sunflower was first cultivated in North and South America. They became popular as flowers when Spanish explorers brought the seeds to Europe in the 1500s. Today, sunflowers are also grown in other parts of the world, including Europe and Asia. Sunflowers thrive when grown in large groups. They need full sun to grow well and develop optimally during the summer months. Sunflower seeds should be planted in fertile, moist, well-drained, thick mulched soil.
